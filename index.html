I’m a PhD student at Mila and the Université de Montréal, advised by Yoshua Bengio. I’m currently visiting Frances Arnold’s lab at Caltech through spring 2026.

My research spans the full lifecycle of generative models — designing efficient training algorithms, getting more out of models at inference time, and making them steerable across flow matching, diffusion, and discrete diffusion. A common thread is sampling: steering or conditioning a model is equivalent to drawing samples from an unnormalized, Boltzmann-like target, a connection I’ve developed for discrete diffusion models (DDPP) and neural samplers (iDEM). I’ve also worked on exploration, including in reinforcement learning and GFlowNets.

I’m especially focused on building strong multimodal generative models that co-design protein sequence and structure, and applying them to enzyme design. In DISCO, our model designs proteins around arbitrary small molecules and — given only a reactive intermediate, with no catalytic residues specified in advance — invents new-to-nature enzymes that catalyze carbene-transfer chemistry nature never evolved, with the best designs matching or exceeding state-of-the-art engineered enzymes.

Much of this has been joint work with close collaborators — among them Alexander Tong, Cheng-Hao Liu, and Esmerelda Whittamer — and many generous people across Mila, Caltech, and beyond. I previously co-founded Dreamfold. Before Mila I grew up in small-town Michigan and graduated from the University of Michigan in 2017 with a BS in Computer Science.

What I work on

Generative models

Designing computationally efficient generative models — flow matching, diffusion, and beyond.

The sampling problem

Sampling from unnormalized densities, including amortized inference and energy-based methods like iDEM.

Molecular & protein design

Using generative models for the de novo design of proteins and other molecular structures.

GFlowNets

Exploration, multi-objective optimization, and credit assignment for amortized sampling of diverse candidates.
